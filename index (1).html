Economics · Data Science · ECO225 · 2026
Mobility, Economic Conditions, and Crime During COVID-19: Evidence from Boston
Using 400,000+ Boston Police Department incident records (2018–2022) merged with FRED labor market data and Google Community Mobility Reports, this paper tests whether mobility restrictions or economic distress better explain the sharp decline in crime at the onset of COVID-19 lockdowns. OLS regressions, regression trees, and a 200-estimator random forest all converge on the same conclusion: the time trend and mobility disruption dominate, while the COVID dummy alone has near-zero predictive power — consistent with routine activity theory over the classic economic distress channel.
